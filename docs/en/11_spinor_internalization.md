# §14: Internalisation of the $\mathbf{16}$ Representation Correspondence and Explicit Statement of Identification Postulates

This document separates the spinor-side argument that was delegated externally in §11.5 into the part that can be written using established mathematics and the part that must be stated explicitly as identification postulates, assigning numbers to the latter.

## 1. Parts Expressible in Established Mathematics

Cayley–Dickson tower and Clifford-algebra periodicity: the 8-periodicity (Bott) of real Clifford algebras shares a common root with Wall's theorem $\mathrm{sBr}(\mathbb{R}) \cong \mathbb{Z}/8$, and belongs to the same tower that §5 used to determine $n^{\ast} = 11$.

Spinor representation: the complex half-spinor representations of $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ are $\Delta^{\pm}$, each 16-dimensional. This is a fact of representation theory, not a choice.

Branching rules: under $\mathfrak{so}(10) \supset \mathfrak{su}(5) \supset \mathfrak{su}(3) \oplus \mathfrak{su}(2) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$, one has $\mathbf{16} = \mathbf{1} \oplus \bar{\mathbf{5}} \oplus \mathbf{10}$, and the hypercharge is uniquely fixed by the branching rules up to normalisation of the abelian direction.

Uniqueness of the abelian direction: by Theorem 6.10.3, the spectrum $\{0, \pm 1, \pm 2\}$ of the centre of the flag stabiliser $U(2)$ on $\mathrm{Im}\,\mathbb{O}$ agrees exactly with the lepton pattern, and the normalisation is determined by integer normalisation. The lepton components of $\bar{\mathbf{5}} \oplus \mathbf{1}$ — namely $L: Y = -1$ and $\nu^{c}: Y = 0$ — and $e^{c}: Y = +2$ in $\mathbf{10}$ are consistent including the conjugates of this assignment.

Consequently, once the identification postulates are granted, all remaining structure is a theorem.

## 2. Identification Postulates (numbered)

Postulate R1 (matter identification): one physical generation of fermions is the irreducible half-spinor module $\Delta^{+}$ of $\mathfrak{so}(10)$.

Status: independent axiom. The representation theory of the tower guarantees the existence of $\Delta^{+}$ but does not force matter to be identified with it.

Postulate R2 (chirality identification): chirality is the grading by $\sigma$-parity.

Status: independent axiom. The odd-parity consistency of §6.8 is supporting evidence but not a proof.

Postulate R3 (abelian-direction identification): the hypercharge direction is the centre of the flag stabiliser.

Status: elevated to theorem (Theorem 6.10.3; uniqueness follows from the lepton-pattern agreement).

Postulate R4 (generation number): $N_{g} = 3$.

Status: not derived. The present theory does not predict the generation number. Theorem 11.5.3 concerns the number of annihilating species $k = 1$ and is not a derivation of the generation number. Any passage that can be read as claiming this has been derived requires correction.

## 3. Conclusions

Once R1 and R2 are added to the axiom inventory (as subdivisions of A9), the representation content of one Standard Model generation, charge quantisation, and the group structure $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ are all theorems. R4 remains explicitly as an open problem. The delegated structure is resolved, and the dependencies are made visible as axioms.
