# §12: Formalisation of Axiom Ω and Complete Inventory of Assumptions

← [Appendices A–E](appendix.md) | Next → [§13: Coincidence Rarity Audit](10_coincidence_audit.md)

This document reconstructs the derivation chain of the theory as a formal system, auditing each step to determine whether it is a theorem, a definition, or an independent axiom. The aim is to replace the claim of "no assumptions" with a verifiable form.

---

## 1. Definition of the Formal System Ω-calc

### 1.1 Language

Objects: the family of real algebras with involution $A$. The initial object is $\mathbb{R}$.

Basic operation: doubling $\mathcal{D}: A \mapsto A \oplus A\,e$ (Cayley–Dickson construction).

Basic involution: $\sigma$. Induced on each $\mathcal{D}(A)$ as the action exchanging the real-section and imaginary-section pair (dual pair) of each doubling stage.

### 1.2 Axioms

Axiom Ω0 (generation): the initial object $\mathbb{R}$ exists and $\mathcal{D}$ is iterable.

Axiom ND (Ω non-degeneracy): for all dual pairs generated by $\mathcal{D}$, the induced action of $\sigma$ is non-trivial.

These two constitute the entire formal content of Axiom Ω from §2.1. The rules of inference use only ordinary mathematics (theorems on ZFC).

## 3. Conclusions

The minimal axiom set is Ω0, ND, and A1–A9. The precise claim of the theory is therefore rewritten as follows.

Old claim: everything is derived from Axiom Ω alone.

New claim: from Axiom Ω (Ω0 + ND) and nine identification axioms, 21 rows of numerically comparable predictions are derived — $n_{\rm obs}$, $\alpha$, $\Lambda$, six CMB peaks, $r$, $w$, $N_{\rm eff}$, $n_{t}$, $n_{s}$, $R_{\rm dec}$, $z_{\rm dec}$, $H_{0}$, $\Omega_{\Lambda}$, $\Omega_{b}h^{2}$, $T_{\rm CMB}$, $\ell_{A}$ — together with structural results ($n^{\ast} = 11$, Standard Model group structure, deep-UV dimension $D = 2$, $T_{\nu}/T_{\gamma}$, dimension-flow scale $l(3)$). Of the 21 rows, $H_{0}$, $\Omega_{\Lambda}$, and $\ell_{A}$ follow dependently from $\Lambda$, and peaks $k \geq 2$ follow from $\ell_{1}$ and $\Delta\ell$; hence the number of fully independent comparison rows is approximately 13. The ratio of independent outputs per axiom is the measure of the theory's economy, and this is a verifiable claim.

The retraction of the old claim is not a weakening of the theory. A "no assumptions" claim that conceals identification axioms collapses at a single counter-example, whereas the form with an explicit inventory allows each axiom to be individually attacked, improved, or replaced — a scientifically stronger structure.
